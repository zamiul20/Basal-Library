# Shifting in basal

Shifting uses the same concept we use to divide by powers of 10 in everyday life (converting millilitres to litres)

Shifting is just descending the decimal point in positions by the shift (but when the shift is negative, the decimal point ascends)

Example - shifting `<RT 654496.457474` by 5 and -5:
```
by 5:
Descend the decimal point by 5 (equivalent of multiplying the number by (R ^ 1)^5 or (base ^ velocity)^shift)
65449645747.4

by -5:
Ascend the decimal point 5 (equivalent of dividing the number by (R ^ 1)^5 or (base ^ velocity)^shift)
6.54496457474

If the decimal point doesn't exist, place it where it would be if it existed
```

Symbol for shifting - `প`:
```
[shift count] প [target]

e.g.  5 প 654496.457474 = 65449645747.4
     -1 প 654496.457474 = 65449.6457474
```
